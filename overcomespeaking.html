Overcome the Fear of Speaking

❮ ❯

    Have you ever felt so nervous, and you couldn’t say anything? Have you experienced embarrassing moments while speaking in front of the class? These are among the things that we need to conquer to deliver a wonderful speech.

    Public Speaking is easily defined as the art of talking in front of many people. It is a practice that most of us fears to do. We have something in mind, but we are shy enough to reveal it to others. Below are the tips by Coach Ariel Tenorio on how to nail a wonderful speech.

    Once the emcee gave the mic-don’t talk right away. Feel the stage first and establish composure. Remember, you own the show, and the audience should enjoy. Don’t make your nervousness seems obvious.

    Expect the unexpected, be ready to what may come- technical problems, human distraction, etc. No matter what happens learn to bounce back and keep in mind, the show must go on.

    Gain the attention of your audience. Let them engage and participate to what you say. Try having a pause in the middle of your talk and you will see, how you can capture their attention.

    As they always say, the eye is the door to the soul. Having a positive eye contact makes you engaged with your audience. When you look at them in the eye it gives you power. They will respect you more when you are confident.

    Notes, PowerPoints, etc. are your backups. It will guide you throughout your talk. Instead of memorizing it, having an outline will give you a guide of the main topics that you can elaborate.

    Naysayers are those who criticize, object to, or oppose something. Have you experienced that after saying a great statement someone will look at you with a face of disagreement? That’s fine, never let negativity overcome you. Instead, maintain positive eye contact to those who actively listens and smiles at you. These are the people that will uplift you and will keep you move along.

    If you are nervous, change your mindset from fear into excitement. Both feels the same, but being excited is what makes it better. Be aware of your gestures and mannerism. Have a person that will give you a constructive criticism. You can also take a video of yourself while having a speech and there, you can rewatch it and plan for the parts that you need to improve.

    Public speaking is a great tool for your career, it will either uplift you or downgrade you. Learn to speak, overcome your shyness. Perhaps conquering your fear to speak in front of many people could be your first step to success.

    As a future public safety leader, developing our skills in public speaking is one of the very important tools to add on our package. Having this skill will help us encourage the public and guide them to the right path in terms of awareness and other public safety advocacies.
