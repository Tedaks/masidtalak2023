True North - Book Review

The book " True North" was written by George Bill with Peter Sims and Foreword by David Gergen. This striking and spectacular book is a guide to discover your “Authentic Leadership” and 125 leaders talked about their failures and personal tragedies, and how these setbacks shaped them as leaders. They offered their personal stories and their perspectives on leadership.

What I like about this book is that it talks about taking your inspiration and passion for leadership from your life story. As we all have unique life stories shaped by past relationships and events. One of the 125 leaders is Howard Schultz. His father had an accident and lost his job, lost their whole family health coverage and his pregnant mother unable to work. So, his parents borrowed money and avoided bill collectors. Eventually Schultz had vowed to create a different reality for workers if he had the chance, and he thus made Starbucks the first company in the United States to offer health coverage to part-time employees working as little as 20 hours a week. In this case, Schultz’s experience and memories of his father carried him along the path to authentic leadership. Schultz inspirational story is just 1 of the 125 leaders who shared their paths to discovering their True North.

This book is a must-read piece. It could guide you on your path to your destination and helps you stay on track to become the best person and the best team leader you can be. When you find your true north, you discover your authentic self.

REFERENCE:True north: Discover your authentic leadership. (n.d.). Goodreads. https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/107420.True_North
