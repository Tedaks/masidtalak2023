PALE BLUE EYES - BOOK REVIEW

The mystery case of The Pale Blue Eye takes place in the 1980s. Its plot follows seasoned detective Augustus Landor in West Point, New York, as he works with young military cadet Edgar Allan Poe to look into a string of killings at the United States Military Academy. The narrative has a really sinister and enigmatic air to it that keeps you wondering what will happen next.

As a mystery thriller set at a military academy, the plot has a distinctive setting. Detective Augustus Landor, the story's primary character, exudes a sense of mystery and has a somber history. With the passing of his wife and the disappearance of his daughter, you can tell he has a complicated past, which heightens the suspense of the narrative. Young cadet Edgar Allan Poe, whose dualism enhances the chemistry between the two characters, joins him in the hunt for the unidentified murderer, rounding out the cast. One is a shrewd, scarred veteran investigator, and the other is a young, upright, and inquisitive cadet who is eager to discover the case's real cause.

With occasional foreshadowing, the manner the story is written also adds a layer of mystery. This keeps the readers interested and paying close attention to every word since they might provide a clue to the case or reveal how the story will conclude.

The crimes being investigated are cruel on a practically existential level, which makes for an engaging detective thriller that the author ultimately succeeded in creating. This is true not just of the compelling characters and the evocative setting. Louis Bayard makes sure to convey the dreariness and the sense of loss through the words he employs to recount the event, even though the murders are horrific enough to engage the imagination. With its linear writing and unexpected twist that makes you want to read more, this book is a wonderful entry point for anyone interested in dark, thriller, and mystery genres.
