The 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership by John Maxwell - BOOK REVIEW

    The book "The 21 Irrefutable Law of Leadership" was written by John C. Maxwell. This timeless and empowering book shows you that leadership is learned not inherited and that you can become a leader too, if you internalize some of the universal principles at play in any leader-follower-relationship.

    What I like about this book is that it talks about how a leader must always stand on solid ground and stick to the rules you represent. The number one element that empowers a leader to be successful is "trust". Even a CEO needs his employees to trust him, because otherwise, why would they bother working with him for a risky business venture for a long time? According to Maxwell, trust is built mainly on three things: honesty, fairness and good judgement. When leaders think they’re above the rules, trouble follows – take Richard Nixon, for example, who thought legal rules wouldn’t apply to him and deemed an illegal break-in as an okay thing to set up. That is only one of the many great points you can get from this amazing book.

    This is a must-read and it’s important to note John Maxwell’s 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership isn’t a set of rules or guidelines for being a leader. What Maxwell means by “irrefutable laws” is that these laws remain true, whether or not you practice them. The bottom-line in leadership isn't how far we advance ourselves but how far we advance others.


REFERENCE:Miller, H. L. (2022, December 14). The best 21 irrefutable laws of leadership summary. Leaders.com. https://leaders.com/articles/leadership/21-irrefutable-laws-of-leadership/
